PHASE 1: THE INTENSIVE | Weeks 1–3

Week 1 — Writing That Hooks

Sessions:

  • Copywriting fundamentals — what makes a line land
  • The anatomy of a great headline — daily headline drills (10 per day, workshopped)
  • Writing for attention spans: hooks, body, call to action
  • Platform-specific copy: Instagram caption vs. LinkedIn post vs. X thread

Assignment: Write 3 pieces of copy for a real Kampala brand

Week 2 — Platform Mechanics & Audience Growth

Sessions:

  • How each platform actually works: Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, X — algorithms, formats, timing
  • Understanding the difference between reach, engagement, and conversion
  • Content calendars: how to build and maintain a consistent presence
  • Audience research: who are you speaking to, what do they need to hear?

Assignment: Build a 30-day content strategy for an assigned brand

Week 3 — Brand Storytelling & Camera Confidence

Sessions:

  • What makes a company's story worth following — how to find the angle
  • Talking to camera: posture, pacing, eye contact, scripting vs. natural delivery
  • Short-form video: scripting, shooting, and editing a 60-second brand story
  • Guest session: practitioner from Kampala's media or advertising industry

Assignment: Produce and submit a 60-second brand story video

Fellows work in teams of three on real briefs from era92 internally and from partner companies. A Creative mentor reviews all work and gives feedback — but does not do the work for them. This phase is deliberately pressured. Real stakes, real output, real accountability.

Brief Sources

  • era92 internal briefs — content era92 Creative or era92 Elevate actually needs: a social media campaign, a reel series, an email sequence, a content calendar
  • Partner company briefs — 3 to 4 Kampala businesses who agree to receive free content in exchange for being a named training partner and providing real feedback

Team Structure

  • Teams of 3 fellows per brief
  • Each team assigned one era92 Creative mentor (2–3 hours per week commitment)
  • Mentors review drafts, give written feedback, attend one live team session per week
  • Teams present progress mid-phase in a 10-minute internal review

Week 4 — Brief Execution — First Half

Sessions:

  • Brief kickoff: teams receive their brief, meet their partner/client
  • Research sprint: audience analysis, competitor content audit, brand voice review
  • Draft content pieces — copy, captions, video scripts, visuals
  • Mentor review session — feedback and revision

Week 5 — Brief Execution — Final Delivery

Sessions:

  • Refinement based on mentor feedback
  • Final content package produced: minimum 5 deliverables per team
  • Internal progress review: each team presents to the full cohort
  • Client/partner review — real feedback from the business
  • Revise based on client feedback (a professional skill in itself)

The most important output of the fellowship is not a certificate — it is a portfolio. Every fellow leaves with three polished, real-world pieces of work they can show to any employer or client. The week ends in a public showcase event open to Kampala business owners, marketers, and media.

Week 6 — Portfolio Build & Public Showcase

Sessions:

  • Select and polish 3 best pieces from Phases 1 and 2
  • Write one case study per piece: the brief, the approach, the result
  • Professional headshot session and LinkedIn profile update
  • Showcase preparation: each team prepares a 5-minute presentation of their live brief work
  • era92 Signal Showcase — public event, invited audience of Kampala businesses and media
  • Closing ceremony: certificate presentation, incentive announcements
